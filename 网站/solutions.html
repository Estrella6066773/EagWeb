Outline for Building a Transportation Powerhouse

In January 2017, the Ministry of Transport launched the "Smart Transportation Action Plan (2017-2020)" to pilot smart highway systems and enhance regional road management.

By February 2018, efforts focused on integrating road transport, upgrading highway systems with 5G for low-latency communication, and advancing vehicle road coordination.

In May 2019, reforms included abolishing toll stations at provincial expressway borders and implementing electronic toll services.

In September 2019, the "Outline for Building a Transportation Powerhouse" emphasized integrating transportation, energy, and information networks to build advanced infrastructure. It highlighted developing intelligent connected vehicles and fostering a comprehensive industrial chain for autonomous driving and smart transportation systems.

Enhancing Smart Highway Service Capabilities: The Foundation of Vehicle-to-Everything (V2X)

In 2018, China advanced regulations and standards for intelligent connected vehicles:

April: MIIT, Ministry of Public Security, and Ministry of Transport issued management specifications for intelligent connected vehicle road testing.

October: MIIT regulated the 5905–5925MHz frequency band for LTE V2X communication in smart vehicles, ensuring a dedicated bandwidth of 20MHz.

November 17: The National Manufacturing Strategy Leading Group’s Committee on Connected Vehicles signed a framework agreement to strengthen cooperation in V2X standards across automotive, intelligent transportation, communications, and traffic management sectors.

December 27: MIIT’s Science and Technology Department released an action plan for developing the connected vehicle industry. It included plans to deploy LTE-V2X on expressways and urban roads, facilitating vehicle-road coordination and enhancing intelligent infrastructure capabilities integrating sensing, communication, and computing.

Introduction to the Monitoring Platform

Government Supervision Platform

Established following Ministry of Transport directives (JTTF [2016] No. 160), this platform is used by regulatory bodies to oversee and assess road transport management agencies, enterprises, and satellite service providers. Its goals include enhancing dynamic supervision of key operating vehicles, improving data quality, ensuring reliable system operation, and enforcing monitoring responsibilities among road transport entities.

Enterprise Monitoring Platform

Also known as an enterprise video surveillance platform, it is utilized by transport companies for intelligent safety and control over their vehicles. This platform includes functionalities such as proactive safety alert data storage and retrieval, real-time vehicle monitoring, handling of vehicle alarm information, proactive safety situation analysis, driver safety profiles, and management of vehicle installation information. It operates under government supervision platform guidelines.

Third‑Party Safety Monitoring Platform

Also known as a third-party platform, road transport management agencies authorize it to specialized third-party safety monitoring organizations. Utilizing advanced technology, this real-time monitoring platform assists regulatory authorities in conducting 24/7 continuous monitoring of key operating vehicles. It focuses on detecting non-compliant behaviors of drivers, issuing timely alarms, and notifying relevant transportation enterprise leaders and safety managers via SMS, mobile apps, phone calls, and other methods for prompt corrective actions. Additionally, it generates data analysis reports for road transport enterprises and regulatory authorities, aiding in establishing effective rules for identifying and addressing violations while maintaining safety responsibilities.
